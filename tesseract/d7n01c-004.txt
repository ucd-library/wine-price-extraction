Note: Our James Beard, author of the popular ‘Fireside Cook
Book’, is still in France busily engaged on a writing assignment as
well as spending much time learning about the vineyards and
restaurants of that country. He will be back in the shop to serve
you during the first days of November.

 

OW back in Paris after several exciting, informative weeks

tasting my way through the wine cellars of Burgundy,
Bordeaux, Champagne and Alsace . . . the city now awakening
from its August slumber and the signs ‘Fermeture Annuelle’ have
been removed ... the incredibly beautiful view of Notre Dame from
the Tour d’Argent in the daytime (incidentally, Notre Dame is
once again illuminated at night) ... the new smart bar of Claude
Terrail of the Tour d’Argent, which is now open opposite the
George V. A pleasant spot for a drink or light luncheon
lunching with Victor Lanson and Jack Aaron and tasting the
really fine Lanson 1945 at the Coq Hardy, which still remains
one of the more fabulous country spots outside of Paris
Duc de Montesquiou proudly showing me the truly beautiful
antique glass Decanter which will house his fine 23 Year Old
Armagnac. Sherry’s will have this in time for Christmas .
drinking the Royal Chadron Brut 1943 at two large parties and
being proud of the fine reception this wine received (you will find
it at the Madison Avenue shop for only 3.89 the bottle)
