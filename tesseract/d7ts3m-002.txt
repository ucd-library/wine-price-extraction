The finest wines of the Bordeaux region are those of the Médoc,
Graves, Sauternes and Saint-Emilion districts. They are usually kept
in the cask for three years and then bottled at the Chateau or in
Bordeaux.

White wines of the first growth Graves are extremely delicate,
have a light bouquet and much vigor. They are comparatively dry,
with the exception of those which come from the Cérons and
Podensac districts.

Sauternes are sweet, ranging from simple district growths to the
inimitable Chateau d’Yquem, the finest of all naturally sweet wines

and in a class by itself.

In 1855, the greatest growths of clarets were classified as to quality
by an official committee and this classification still holds good. Most
of the wines in this list are mis en bouteilles au chateau, or chateau-
bottled, which simply means that the wine was bottled where it was
made and by the people who made it. It is a certain guarantee of
authenticity but not necessarily of quality.
