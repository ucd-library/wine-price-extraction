BEERENAUSLESE AND

THE MOST FABULOUS AND EXPENSIVE TABLE WINES OF THE WORLD

One glass of these wines represents a dramatic experience —rather like drinking and
smelling a flower garden. The beeren means individual grapes are picked at the precise
moment of ripeness. The trocken means “dry” or those wines that have almost turned
into raisins. Even in Germany good examples of these command anywhere from $20.00
to $60.00 the bottle. The production of these wines is not a matter of profit but of pride
because the proprietor can get much more money for the same acreage when making
his usual wines. These wines are made drop by drop. Here are wines to give as a Christ-
mas present for a dramatic impact and to serve at the end of your holiday dinner instead
of brandy. Quantities obviously are limited.
