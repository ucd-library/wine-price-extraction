RED BURGUNDY An Appraisal of the 1964 Vintage.

In Burgundy, the growers were more fortunate than in Bordeaux for there was no rain during
the harvest and quality, in general, proved excellent. Like the 1959s, the Reds have great
charm and fruit, which was apparent from the very beginning, but the ’64s appear to assert
more character. On the whole, the 1964 red burgundies are firmer, and with more depth and

staying power than the ’59’s, In general, Burgundy, rather than Bordeaux, fared better
in 1964.
