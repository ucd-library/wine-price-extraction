The wines of the “Domaine de la Romanee Conti,” because of
their scarcity and magnificence, have always been collector’s
items among serious wine drinkers. It is therefore no ordinary
event when 16 of its wines of excellent vintage years are offered
by a single wine merchant at such relatively modest prices.
The Domaine de la Romanee Conti, which estate bottles every-
thing it sells, consists of five vineyards; all of Romanee Conti,
all of La Tache, goodly portions of Richebourg, Grand Echezeaux
and Echezeaux. The crowning jewel of the Domaine is the peerless
Romanee Conti vineyard (it consists of only five acres which
brought as much as $56,000 back in 1868 and is consistently the
most expensive red wine on the world market). Almost any of
these wines will prove absolutely superb for immediate drinking,
but since they are the most long lived of red wines, they will
reach even greater heights after they have matured further in your
cellars.
