CHAMPAGNES

Since champagne is generally a blend of the wines of several vine-
yards, and not the product of a single vineyard, the integrity of the
blending firm is of supreme importance. Consequently, we have
listed only champagne houses of unquestioned merit.

The two major classes of champagne are known as “Vintage” and
“Non Vintage.” Non Vintage champagne is a product of wines of
different vineyards, and different years, which are blended together
to average quality and cost.

Vintage champagne is the result of blending wine of different
vineyards but only of a single year which has proven exceptionally
good.

Dryness in champagne is generally indicated by the following
expressions:
“BRUT”—Fraction of 1 percent of sugar added
“EXTRA DRY”—Slightly sweeter than “brut”
“SEC’—Slightly sweeter than “Extra Dry”
“DEMI-SEC’—Sweetest available
