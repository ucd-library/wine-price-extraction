Growths of the Gironde

Only the wines of the Médoc were classified, with the
exception of Haut-Brion from Graves. The other fine red-
wine-producing districts of Saint Emilion, Pomerol and
Graves were overlooked with the result that for more than
one hundred years the wines from these districts suffered
unfairly in prestige and reputation. In addition to the five
classified growths, seven wines were unofficially considered
as “crus exceptionnels” and hundreds of minor chateaux
were unofficially listed but not officially classified as “crus
bourgeois” and “crus artisans.”

In the new classification, the author has upgraded certain

_ deserving “crus exceptionnels” und a few “crus bourgeois”
not included in the official classification, as well as the
chateaux of Saint Emilion, Pomerol and Graves, which
today are producing distinguished wines recognized for their
quality and prestige, as well as by the high prices paid.
