GERMAN WINES

The beautiful. valley of the Rhine produces unexcelled white
wines, inimitable and famous since the days of antiquity. The
valley is divided into three important wine-producing districts, the
Rheingau, the Rheinhesse, and the Palatinate, a little to the south
of the left bank. Wines of the Rheingau are the most celebrated,
although the Rheinhesse produces some very noteworthy wines,
and those of the Palatinate are distinctive with perhaps slightly
more character.

The label of an especially fine wine will bear one of the follow-
ing terms: ‘“‘Auslese’’, a wine made from selected bunches of
grapes; ‘“Spatlese’’, a wine made from grapes gathered late in their
period of ripening.
