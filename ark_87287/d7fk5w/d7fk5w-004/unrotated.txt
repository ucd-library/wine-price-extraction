BURGUNDY

This ancient province of France boasts an equally ancient fame
for its great wines, and in the opinion of many connoisseurs, they
have no equal. Characterized by a full-bodied, rich flavor, fragrant
bouquet and a deep ruby color, the red wines are highly prized and
sought after by gourmets the world over. The white wines are dry,
vigorous and acknowledged as the finest in France.

The great difficulty confronting the buyer of Burgundies is the
bewildering array of wines bearing the same name, but having no
resemblance to each other in quality. This is due to the fact that
the vineyards of Burgundy are parcelled out among many owners,
some of them growers, others traders, and not all of these
make the wine which carries the name of their vineyard. There-
fore in Burgundy, the important factor is the integrity of the grower
and the dealer from whom the wine is obtained.
