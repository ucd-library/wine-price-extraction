The white wines of the Loire Valley are celebrated for their
fresh, fruity flavor with their most notable characteristic—a sweet-
ness held in check. Vouvray and Saumur are two most agreeable
table wines, particularly at luncheon, and are becoming increasingly
popular in America. The sparkling varieties have a delicacy which
makes them analogous to Champagne, though for most cultivated
tastes, they are a trifle too sweet.

Wines from Alsace and Lorraine are noteworthy and are com-
monly grouped together as Alsatian wines. These generally re-
semble the Rhine wines of Germany and make pleasant dry
luncheon wines, especially with seafood.
